Medical Images

While similar in principle to natural image processing, these images often have more subtle differences than natural images. Further, the potential social impact of models that can classify X-Ray Images with success speaks for itself.

Pretraining

CheXpert

The CheXpert dataset contains over 200K chest radiographs from over 65K patients. These images are used for automated chest x-ray interpretation. The radiographs and associated radiology reports were obtained from Stanford Hospital. The dataset includes uncertainty labels and radiologist-labeled reference standard evaluation sets. Based on prevalence and clinical relevance 14 observations were chosen for labeling as positive, negative, or uncertain.

Transfer

X-ray 8

The X-ray 8 dataset contains over 100K frontal-view chest x-ray images from over 30K patients, including many with advanced disease. Using text-mining, eight disease image labels were taken from the associated radiological reports. Each image can have multiple labels.
